Xizi Wang

Ph.D student, Computer Science, Indiana University
My research objective is to teach computer to "Look, Listen & Understand". I am interested in multi-modal video learning, audio-video LLM, action understanding and designing models for practical applications in multiple disciplinaries.

Work Experience

  • Research Assistant, Indiana University, 08/2020 - Present

    TimeRefine: Temporal Grounding with Time Refining Video LLM

    • Propose TimeRefine that can be easily integrated to any LLM-based temporal grounding model
    • Enhance the temporal grounding model by incorporating temporal refinement and encouraging self-correction from previous predictions
    • In submission to CVPR 2025

    Active Speaker Detection

    • Propose an end-to-end long-term multi-speaker network that leverages both audio and visual signals
    • Applied in EngageAI and Ikigai Robot Project with Toyota Research Institute
    • Accepted by CVPR 2024

    Large-scale video language Pretraining

    • Explore the important factors in Video-and-Language model design, and provide a step-by-step recipe (VINDLU) for effective pretraining
    • Validation of VINDLU on several Text-to-Video Retrieval and Video Question Answering benchmarks
    • Accepted by CVPR 2023

    Traffic Anomaly Detection in Egocentric Videos

    • Propose an unsupervised method using future object localization (FOL) to detect anomalous events from egocentric videos by predicting future locations of traffic participants.
    • Propose a novel spatial-temporal area under curve (STAUC) evaluation metric.
    • Accepted by TPAMI
